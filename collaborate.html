Talks, workshops, and collaboration

I work with academic and industry groups on human–computer interaction, human–AI systems, and interactive systems design. Engagements are typically scoped around learning goals and responsible use—not product hype.

Formats

Invited talks (about 45–60 minutes) on research themes such as workflow-grounded HCI, mixed-initiative interaction, creativity support, and generative interfaces.

Workshops (half-day or full-day) for teams who want structured discussion and hands-on critique: mapping workflows, defining human–AI division of labor, and identifying evaluation criteria that match real professional risk.

Example topics

  • Human–AI interaction for expert workflows (agency, accountability, repair)
  • Designing interactive systems with empirical discipline (what to build, what to measure)
  • Generative tools without eroding domain judgment
  • From prototypes to evidence: technical HCI in practice

What helps before we schedule

A short brief on audience (researchers, designers, engineers, leadership), desired outcomes, and any constraints (tools, data, compliance) makes sessions substantially more useful.

Contact

For research partnerships and speaking inquiries, email xac@ucla.edu. For student opportunities, see the UCLA HCI group.

Ethics and boundaries

I do not provide ad hoc legal or clinical advice. Industry workshops focus on frameworks, critique, and research-backed guidance; product decisions remain the host organization’s responsibility.
