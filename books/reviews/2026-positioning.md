Nonetheless, this book is an example of a viable approach to putting together a book—through a series of essays on the same topic.

The table of contents is also a great example—each chapter comes with a concise summary.

Some notes/quotes:

**What is positioning**

Overall, positioning is about answering the question: how are you different?

> ... positioning is not what you do to a product. ... you position the product in the mind of the prospect.

The book talks about how the first to enter a market has immense power over subsequent players. However, in the tech world, there have been some counterexamples:
- Yahoo was the first to enter the search engine market, but Google came later and took over;
- OpenAI was the first to enter the LLM chatbot market, but others (Google Gemini, Anthropic Claude, xAI Grok) followed suit and pretty much evened the game.

The chapter about Jamaica is worth revisiting—a very nice example showing that positioning is as important as, if not more important than, the substance.
