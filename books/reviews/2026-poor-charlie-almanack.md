This book is a collection of speeches by Charlie Munger delivered on various occasions, such as college commencements and philanthropic gatherings.

One key takeaway is the use of checklists. People might assume that Munger and his partner-in-crime Warren Buffett rely on some special, complicated scheme to achieve their success. In reality, what Munger practices is maintaining a checklist of “rules of thumb.” For example, when assessing whether a company is worth investing in, he goes through this checklist, which I imagine systematically (albeit empirically) identifies reasons to invest or not.

This checklist strategy is quite compelling. It suggests that life does not require a complex model, but rather a curated set of simple, atomic models that consistently work.

The book itself is a testament to this philosophy. Munger’s talks are essentially repeated rehearsals of his checklist of life wisdom. You hear the same phrases multiple times throughout the book.

Since I listened to the book without taking notes, I asked an LLM to help compile a list of Munger’s well-known wisdom. Here they are:

### **1) “To a man with a hammer, everything looks like a nail.”**
**Meaning:** If you only know one model, you’ll misuse it everywhere.  
**Use it:** Build a _latticework of mental models_ across disciplines.

---

### **2) “Invert, always invert.”**

**Meaning:** Don’t just ask “How do I succeed?” Ask “How do I fail?” and avoid that.  
**Use it:** List the ways your plan could break—then systematically remove them.

---

### **3) “All I want to know is where I’m going to die, so I’ll never go there.”**

**Meaning:** Avoiding big mistakes matters more than chasing brilliance.  
**Use it:** Risk management > cleverness.

---

### **4) “It’s not supposed to be easy. Anyone who finds it easy is stupid.”**

**Meaning:** If something is obviously easy, you’re probably missing something.  
**Use it:** Expect difficulty; treat “easy wins” with suspicion.

---

### **5) “Spend each day trying to be a little wiser than you were when you woke up.”**

**Meaning:** Compounding isn’t just financial—it’s intellectual.  
**Use it:** Daily marginal improvement beats bursts of intensity.

---

### **6) “Take a simple idea and take it seriously.”**

**Meaning:** Most people under-execute obvious truths.  
**Use it:** Depth over novelty—push basic ideas further than others.

---

### **7) “Show me the incentive and I’ll show you the outcome.”**

**Meaning:** Incentives drive behavior—often more than stated values.  
**Use it:** When analyzing any system, start with incentives.

---

### **8) “Knowing what you don’t know is more useful than being brilliant.”**

**Meaning:** Self-awareness beats raw IQ.**Use it:** Stay inside your _circle of competence_.

---

### **9) “The big money is not in the buying and selling, but in the waiting.”**

**Meaning:** Patience is a strategic advantage.  
**Use it:** Avoid activity for activity’s sake.

---

### **10) “A great business at a fair price is superior to a fair business at a great price.”**

**Meaning:** Quality compounds; cheapness often doesn’t.  
**Use it:** Don’t optimize for price alone—optimize for durability.

---

### **11) “If you don’t get the elementary probability into your repertoire, you go through a long life like a one-legged man in an ass-kicking contest.”**

**Meaning:** Misunderstanding probability wrecks decisions.  
**Use it:** Think in odds, base rates, and expected value.

---

### **12) “People calculate too much and think too little.”**

**Meaning:** Over-modeling can hide poor judgment.  
**Use it:** Balance math with clear reasoning.

---

### **13) “Mimicking the herd invites regression to the mean.”**

**Meaning:** Following the crowd rarely produces exceptional outcomes.  
**Use it:** Independent thinking is required for outperformance.

---

### **14) “The first rule of compounding: never interrupt it unnecessarily.”**

**Meaning:** Friction (taxes, fees, switching) destroys long-term gains.  
**Use it:** Let good processes run.

---

### **15) “You don’t have to be brilliant, only a little bit wiser than the other guys… over a long time.”**

**Meaning:** Consistency beats flashes of genius.  
**Use it:** Design for long-term edge, not short-term wins.
